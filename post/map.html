Makerspace, Livingston Campus

SCORE: 94

The Rutgers Makerspace hosts a bunch of equipment including 3D printers, laser cutters/engravers, woodshop, textiles, and more. It's kind of like using the bathroom in your friend's house. Not a close friend, but perhaps one you met in a group project and were then forced to interact with obligation.

Tillet Hall, Livingston Campus

SCORE: 95

Tillet Hall - home to classrooms, great study spots, and the occasional dance practice. Not the prettiest bathrooms, but you'll get the job done.

Recreation Center, Livingston Campus

SCORE: 90

Livingston Recreation Center is home to friendly staff, open areas for sports, and way more exercise equipment than you know what to do with. I promised myself I'd go to the gym more often and then didn't visit here a single time during my last semester. Whoops.

Library, Livingston Campus

SCORE: 98

Our resident James Dickson Carr Library contains a lot of books, I think. I've never actually picked one up to read it. They could all be blank for all I know. Bathrooms are pretty nice, though!

Livingston Student Center, Livingston Campus

SCORE: 84

The Livingston Student Center contains multitudes. I'm still shocked Rutgers had the budget for a mostly functioning, free arcade after they slashed the budget on like, every single club. Not Cheese Club, though. They got like $20k for one festival. Anyways, the student center is a great place for clubs of all budgets to meet.

Dining Commons, Livingston Campus

SCORE: 78

Largely considered the best of all the dining halls, Livingston Dining Hall serves up extremely adequate food. Its overall quality ceiling is higher than most, but it's still college cafeteria food... Not going to miss it when I leave.

Towers, Livingston Campus

SCORE: 33

I have heard HORRIBLE stories about the Lynton Towers North dorms. My friend basically got every single type of airborne lung disease their freshman year living here. And Covid, on top of that. Surely there must be a solution here?
